Visualizing Baseball Statistics Using Crossfilter.js (back to top)

The charts displayed below result from the multi-dimensional filtering mechanisms that come with Crossfilter.js. The data being visualized comes from Lahman's dataset on how many all-star games certain baseball players have appeared in over the years. Everytime one of the sliders is adjusted, the data is filtered accordingly across all dimensions and the charts are re-rendered and updated to reflect the new subset. Two drop-downs also allow filtering on teams by their alphabetical arrangement and drilling down on the first 10 players in the data set. More information about Lahman's dataset can be found here. As previously mentioned, the library used is Crossfilter.js, Version 1.3.12.

The Biological Variations of Iris', Examined through DC.js and Crossfilter (back to top)

This visualization shows the variable species, sepal lengths and widths, and petal lengths and widths of iris flowers. Each chart is linked together, so selecting a subset of one or multiple dimensions will filter and update the other dimensions accordingly. For convenience, a reset button becomes available whenever a chart is filtered, allowing a quick return to the original state. The API used is here. The libraries utilized include:
DC.js, Version: 1.7.5
Underscore.js, Version: 1.8.3
Crossfilter.js, Version: 1.3.12

The Evolution of Ebola, 2014-2016: A Story in C3.js (back to top)

Ebola took a massive toll on African countries, particularly in recent years. This visualization examines the number of Ebola case from 2014 to present day, how it has evolved, and the severity of the disease in various nations. The charts highlight the staggering numbers and notable trends. The data used was pulled from here. Libraries used include:
D3.js, Version 3.5.6
C3.js, Version 0.4.10

Customize Your Donut with D3.js (back to top)

Taking a donut dataset of varied options (e.g. toppings, batters, fillings, etc.), this fun visualization creates a hierarchy based on the data definition. It allows for expanding and contracting nodes to either show or hide more options, and does so with a neat radial effect that rotates and transforms the visualization based on user interaction. This was created with D3.js, Version 3.5.6.
